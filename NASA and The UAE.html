    What Does NASA Do?

NASA or The National Aeronautics and Space Administration is America’s space program and is the global leader of space exploration. They are responsible for science and technology related to air and space. NASA also works on different projects with different countries to enhance exploration in space for humans and robots. They are also responsible for the ISS (International Space Station). At their facilities they study Earth, its climate, our Sun, and our solar system and beyond! They are conducting research, testing and developing things to advance aeronautics, including electric propulsion and supersonic flight. They develop and fund space technologies that will help in future exploration and benefit life on Earth. NASA also leads a Moon to Mars exploration approach, which includes working with US industry, international partners and academia to develop new technology, and send research and soon humans to explore the moon on different missions that will help prepare for human exploration on Mars (The Red Planet). Besides that, companies use NASA discoveries and technologies to create products for ordinary people like you and me. So, they can ensure success for the agency and the world, NASA also supports education efforts in STEM to increase future workforce.

    What are some projects that NASA have done besides space and technology?

NASA has done many things for the benefit of mankind here are some examples:

Artificial Limbs- NASA had originally designed these for space vehicles but hey make artificial human limbs which are more functionable, durable, comfortable and life-like.

Scratch-resistant lenses- After NASA developed scratch resistant astronaut helmets the agency continues experimenting with scratch-resistant plastics which now compromise of scratch resistant shades and lenses.

LASIK- Technology used to track astronauts' eyes during periods in space in order to assess how humans' frames of reference are affected by weightlessness has become essential for use during LASIK surgery. The device tracks a patient's eye positions for the surgeon.

Water Filtration- In the 1970s, NASA developed filtration systems that used iodine and cartridge filters to ensure that astronauts had access to safe, tasteless water. This filtering technology is now very common.

3D Food Printing- The ability to cook food on long space missions is no longer impossible with the invention of 3D food printers! This technology is now being unfortunately for commercial use for the production of chocolates and other confections as well as to create nutritious foods for diabetics and others with specific needs.

    What inventions have NASA made for their space missions?

Infrared thermometer R5/Valkyrie
The infrared sensors were developed by NASA for the measurements of the temperature of distant celestial objects. This technology led to the development of optical sensor thermometers which have provided us with body temperature readings when placed inside our ear canals. NASA engineers built a humanoid robot called Valkyrie or Robonaut 5 (R5) to help in a crewed mission to Mars or the Moon. R5 could assemble equipment before astronauts arrive and do maintenance or repairs after the crew arrives. R5 was initially designed to complete disaster-relief maneuvers. However, its main goal is to prove itself worthy of even trickier terrain- deep space exploration. This robot might just make breakthroughs in humanoid control, motion planning and perception. This robot also cost $2 million and hopefully this robot will help us humans colonize Mars.
